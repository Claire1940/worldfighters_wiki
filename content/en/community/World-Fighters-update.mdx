## Speed Modifiers: Accelerating the Grind

One of the most impactful features of the 1.75 patch is the introduction of speed modifiers for game modes like Dragon Defense and Tempest Invasions. Previously, these modes could feel like a significant time sink, especially when players were strong enough to clear waves effortlessly but were limited by the fixed movement speed of enemies.

Players can now toggle between different speed settings to complete invasions significantly faster. This is particularly useful for those farming tokens to max out their secret gachas.

| Modifier Level | Cost per Run | Effect | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1x Speed** | Standard Key | Default enemy movement | Testing new builds or lower power levels. |
| **2x Speed** | +1 Specific Key (e.g., Slime/Saying) | Enemies move 200% faster | Standard grinding for tokens and shards. |
| **3x Speed** | Gems | Enemies move 300% faster | High-tier players looking to burn through keys. |

> **💡 Tip:** To use the 2x speed modifier in Dragon Defense, ensure you have multiple "Saying Keys" in your inventory. The speed increase consumes an additional key but effectively halves your completion time, making it the most efficient way to farm overnight.

## Quality of Life: Streamlining the User Interface

The developers have prioritized "Quality of Life" (QoL) in this update, addressing several community complaints regarding the game's interface and inventory management. The addition of confirmation boxes for deleting high-rarity items is perhaps the most celebrated change, as it prevents the accidental loss of Mythic or Secret swords and accessories.

### Automated Systems and UI Enhancements
The UI has seen several subtle but powerful upgrades. The "Auto Claim Rewards" feature allows players to stay focused on combat without manually clicking through reward menus every few minutes. Additionally, the new "Drop Billboards" placed next to game modes provide immediate transparency regarding what loot is available, saving players from having to check external wikis constantly.

| Feature | Description | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Auto Claim** | Automatically collects time-based and milestone rewards. | Essential for AFK macro-grinding sessions. |
| **Hide Gacha** | Option to disable the summon animation entirely. | Speeds up mass-summoning sessions for secrets. |
| **Teleport Return** | Automatically returns you to your previous spot after a raid. | Prevents getting lost or having to run back to farm spots. |
| **Passives Select** | A dedicated button for 100% crafted passives. | Simplifies the gear optimization process. |

## Combat Testing and the New Damage Dummy

For players obsessed with min-maxing their DPS, the 1.75 **World Fighters update** introduces a Damage Test Dummy. Located conveniently in front of the leaderboards, this dummy features infinite health and a live DPS tracker.

This tool is essential for testing the effectiveness of different sword and accessory combinations. As players reach the late-game stages where Secret-rarity items become the norm, understanding the actual damage output versus the paper stats is crucial.

### Testing Your Build
When using the dummy, pay attention to your "Peak DPS" versus your "Sustained DPS." The current meta suggests that hitting a peak of approximately 74.0 is a benchmark for high-level progression in the Slime and Dragon arenas. If your numbers are falling short, consider focusing on your accessory rarity, as moving from Mythic to Secret accessories provides the largest percentage boost to base damage in the current 2026 meta.

| Accessory Name | Rarity | Drop Source | Notable Stats |

> **⚠️ Warning:** While the 2x speed modifier makes waves go faster, it also means enemies reach your base or defensive line twice as quickly. Do not use speed modifiers if you are struggling to clear waves at 1x speed, as you may fail the encounter and waste your keys.

## Bug Fixes and Performance Stability

Beyond the flashy new features, the 1.75 patch addresses several lingering bugs that affected high-level play. The "Enemy Attacking Delay" fix is particularly important for players pushing high waves in dungeons, where a single frame of lag or a delayed enemy hit could result in an unexpected death.

**Resolved Issues in Update 1.75:**
*   **Notification Stacking:** UI notifications no longer overlap, making it easier to read loot drops during intense combat.
*   **Renaming Tokens:** Fixed formatting errors when players attempted to rename their specific tokens for organization.
*   **Diablo Animations:** The animation glitch involving the Diablo sword and specific unit stances has been smoothed out for better visual clarity.
*   **Last Position Memory:** The game now reliably teleports you back to your exact coordinates after exiting a game mode, which is a massive fix for those who utilize auto-clickers in specific world locations.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the 3x speed modifier in World Fighters?**

A: Unlike the 2x speed which costs an extra key, the 3x speed modifier currently requires Gems. It is designed for players who have an abundance of premium currency and want to clear raids in the absolute minimum amount of time.

**Q: Where is the best place to farm keys for the new speed modifiers?**

A: The Dragon Arena remains the premier location for farming keys. By setting your character to auto-farm the arena overnight, you can easily accumulate enough keys to run 2x speed Dragon Defense missions throughout the following day.

**Q: What are the drop odds for the Secret Baron Naruto unit?**

A: Currently, the game displays the drop odds for Baron Naruto as a "question mark" (?). Community testing suggests the rate is extremely low, likely sub-0.1%, as many players report not seeing a single drop even after a full night of AFK macro-grinding.

**Q: Does the "Auto Claim" feature reset my daily rewards?**

A: No, the Auto Claim feature only collects rewards as they become available. You must still manually trigger the "Reset Rewards" option if you wish to start a new cycle of milestone achievements.

**Q: Can I use speed modifiers in all game modes?**

A: As of the **World Fighters update** 1.75, speed modifiers are primarily available for Dragon Defense and Tempest Invasions. There are plans to roll this feature out to other trial modes in future patches.
