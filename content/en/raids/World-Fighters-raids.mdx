## New Speed Modifiers for Raids and Invasions

One of the most impactful additions in the 1.75 update is the introduction of speed modifiers for specific game modes. This feature is a game-changer for veteran players who have already optimized their damage output and find the standard wave timings too slow. Currently, these modifiers apply to the Dragon Defense and Tempest Invasions, which are the primary **World Fighters raids** players use to farm tokens and keys.

| Modifier Level | Cost (Dragon Defense) | Cost (Slime Invasion) | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1x Speed** | Free (Standard Key) | Free (Standard Key) | Default progression speed. |
| **2x Speed** | **1 Saying Key** | **1 Slime Key** | Enemies move and spawn 100% faster. |
| **3x Speed** | **Gems** | **Gems** | Maximum efficiency for high-DPS builds. |

> **💡 Tip:** Only activate 3x speed if your units can one-shot enemies at the spawn point. If your kill time is slower than the spawn rate, you may lose out on rewards by being overwhelmed.

To activate these modifiers, you must interact with the game mode billboard and select the desired speed before starting the match. For F2P players, the 2x speed is the "sweet spot," as it only requires an additional key which can be easily farmed in the Dragon Arena.

## Optimizing the Slime Invasion Raid

The Slime Invasion remains a staple for players seeking to upgrade their accessories. Unlike the Dragon Defense, which focuses heavily on gacha tokens, the Slime Invasion is the go-to source for high-tier cosmetic and stat-boosting items. With the 2026 updates, the drop billboards next to the game mode now clearly display what you are fighting for.

### Slime Invasion Drop Rates and Rewards

| Item Name | Rarity | Type | Drop Chance/Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Admiral Cape** | Mythic/Secret | Accessory | Rare Drop from Waves 15+ |
| **Cute Slime** | Mythic | Accessory | Rare Drop from Waves 10+ |
| **Fire Hat** | Mythic | Accessory | Rare Drop from Waves 10+ |

## Quality of Life Changes in Update 1.75

While the speed modifiers are the headline feature, several "Quality of Life" (QoL) improvements have made the daily loop of **World Fighters raids** significantly less frustrating. These changes target the UI and inventory management, which were previously major bottlenecks for active players.

*   **Auto-Claim Rewards:** You can now toggle an auto-claim feature for your playtime and quest rewards. This ensures you never miss out on gems or keys while you are AFK grinding.
*   **Hide Gacha Animations:** A long-requested feature, this allows you to burn through thousands of tokens in seconds. This is essential after a long night of raiding when you have a massive backlog of summons to perform.
*   **Teleport to Last Position:** When you finish a game mode or raid, the game now teleports you back to your exact previous coordinates. This is perfect for players who macro their movement to stand in front of specific NPCs or raid portals.
*   **Confirmation Prompts:** The game now asks for confirmation before you delete Swords, Units, or Accessories. This prevents accidental loss of Secret rarity items during inventory cleanouts.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The "Auto-Claim" feature does not automatically reset the reward board. You must still manually reset the board once all rewards are claimed to start the timer for the next set.

## Preparing for High-Level Content: The Test Dummy

Before jumping into the more difficult **World Fighters raids**, it is vital to know exactly how much damage your current build is producing. The 1.75 update added a dedicated Damage Test Dummy located directly in front of the leaderboards.

Using the test dummy allows you to calculate your DPS (Damage Per Second) without the chaos of a live raid environment. This is particularly useful for testing the effectiveness of new Secret swords or Mythic accessories.

### How to Use the Test Dummy Effectively
1.  **Clear your buffs:** Start with a clean slate to see your base damage.
2.  **Toggle Auto-Attack:** Engage the dummy and watch the DPS counter.
3.  **Equip Passives:** Swap your crafted passives to see which ones provide the highest multiplicative boost.
4.  **Check Range:** Move away from the dummy to see at what distance your damage begins to fall off or stop entirely.

A DPS peaking around 74.0 is considered a strong baseline for mid-to-late game raiding in 2026, though top-tier players often reach much higher numbers with fully optimized Secret sets.

## The Dragon Defense Strategy

Dragon Defense is the primary method for obtaining the tokens required to max out your secret gachas. To grind this effectively, follow the "One and a Half Night" strategy used by top players.

1.  **Farm Keys:** Spend time in the Dragon Arena to accumulate at least 50-100 Saying Keys.
2.  **Macro Setup:** Use an auto-clicker or macro to enter the Dragon Defense portal automatically.
3.  **Speed Toggle:** If you have the keys to spare, always run at 2x speed. This cuts your grinding time in half.
4.  **Vault Management:** Ensure your vault has enough space for the influx of items. With the new confirmation prompts, you can safely mass-delete the lower-tier loot after your session.

By following this loop, you can earn enough tokens in roughly 36 hours of AFK time to fully max out your gacha progress for the current world.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get more keys for World Fighters raids?**

A: The most reliable way to get keys is by farming the corresponding Arena (e.g., Dragon Arena for Saying Keys). You can also obtain them through the daily rewards board, which can now be set to auto-claim in the settings menu.

**Q: Is the 3x speed modifier worth the gem cost?**

A: For most players, 2x speed using a key is more cost-effective. 3x speed is generally reserved for "power-grinding" during limited-time events or when you have an abundance of gems and need to clear waves as fast as humanly possible.

**Q: Why am I not getting the Baron Naruto drop in the trial?**

A: The drop rates for Secret units like Baron Naruto are extremely low and often hidden behind a "???" indicator in the game UI. Even with a full night of AFK grinding, it is common not to see a single drop. Increasing your Luck stat through accessories and potions is highly recommended.

**Q: Does the speed modifier affect the drop rate of items?**

A: No, the speed modifier only affects how fast enemies move and spawn. It does not change the percentage chance of an item dropping, but because you are clearing waves faster, you are essentially getting more "rolls" at the loot table per hour.
