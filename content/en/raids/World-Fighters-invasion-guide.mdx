## Core Aircraft Categories and Roles

The foundation of any successful invasion lies in knowing which tool to use for the job. In the current 2026 meta, aircraft are broadly divided into air superiority, strike capability, and reconnaissance units. Each serves a specific purpose in your broader strategic goals.

### Air Superiority Fighters
These are your primary defensive and offensive tools for clearing the skies. Their sole mission is to hunt down and destroy enemy planes, bombers, and helicopters. While they can technically target ground or naval units, their damage output in those areas is negligible.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never fly your air superiority fighters directly over enemy SAM (Surface-to-Air Missile) launchers or Cruisers. These units will engage your planes even if you aren't attacking them directly.

### Strike Fighters
If air superiority fighters clear the path, strike fighters finish the job. These units are designed for ground-pound missions, dealing massive damage to infantry and armored divisions. They are the "workhorses" of any early-to-mid-game invasion force.

| Unit Type | Primary Target | Main Weakness | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Air Superiority** | Enemy Aircraft | SAM Launchers / Cruisers | Protecting ground stacks and clearing bombers. |
| **Strike Fighter** | Infantry / Tanks | Air Superiority Fighters | Eliminating ground units in the early game. |
| **Stealth Fighter** | High-Value Targets | Max-level SAMs | Surprise attacks and deep-territory scouting. |
| **UAV (Drone)** | Intelligence | Any Anti-Air | Scouting enemy cities without declaring war. |

## Mastering the Patrol Mechanic

One of the most underutilized features in the World Fighters invasion guide is the Patrol function. Instead of simply ordering a "Direct Attack," patrolling allows your aircraft to circle a specific area and automatically engage any threats that enter their radius.

1.  **Defensive Patrol:** Place Air Superiority Fighters over your own cities. This makes them virtually invulnerable to enemy bombers.
2.  **Offensive Cover:** When your ground troops move into enemy territory, have your fighters patrol directly above them. They will automatically intercept enemy helicopters or strike fighters attempting to harass your land forces.
3.  **Intel Gathering:** Patrolling near borders with stealth units allows you to see enemy movements without triggering an automatic declaration of war (provided you don't cross the line).

## Stealth and Naval Variations

As the game progresses toward the mid-2026 stages, standard fighters often fall off in effectiveness due to the proliferation of mobile anti-air units. This is where specialized variants come into play.

### Stealth Capabilities
Stealth aircraft are invisible on radar unless they are actively attacking or fly within range of specialized detection units (like max-level SAMs or other stealth fighters). This allows you to fly through "closed" airspace to scout enemy unit compositions or launch devastating surprise attacks on grounded airfields.

### Naval Fighters
While Naval Air Superiority and Strike Fighters offer the ability to land on aircraft carriers, they are generally considered less cost-effective than their land-based counterparts. The extra research time and resource investment often outweigh the benefits of carrier-based operations, especially since carriers themselves are vulnerable to submarines and heavy cruisers.

> **💡 Tip:** Stick to land-based fighters unless you are playing a map with heavy island-hopping requirements where airfields are scarce.

## The Role of UAVs in Invasion Planning

Information is the most valuable resource in any conflict. Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) are your primary tools for gathering this information. Early-level UAVs are visible and can be shot down easily, but once they reach their stealth upgrades, they become nearly impossible to detect.

*   **Homeland Spying:** Send maxed-out UAVs to patrol over enemy capital cities. This reveals exactly what buildings they have and what units they are currently mobilizing.
*   **Border Watch:** Keep a UAV on your borders to detect incoming "stacks" before they reach your territory.
*   **Avoid Combat:** UAVs deal almost no damage. Using them to attack is a waste of a valuable reconnaissance asset.

## Building the Ultimate Air Force Stack

To execute a flawless invasion, you need a balanced production schedule. Following this World Fighters invasion guide timeline will give you a significant edge over opponents who build units haphazardly.

| Phase | Priority Research | Production Goal | Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|

### Why Transition in the Late Game?
By Day 25 or 30, most competent players will have researched and deployed Mobile SAM Launchers and Cruisers. At this stage, relying solely on aircraft becomes a liability. Your World Fighters invasion guide strategy should shift toward using your air force as a support wing for a heavy ground or naval force. Use your planes to pick off isolated units, but avoid flying into the "death zones" created by overlapping anti-air envelopes.

## Counter-Play and Engagement Tactics

Understanding how to read an engagement report is vital. If your planes take unexpected damage, check the report to see if the enemy had "Engaged Anti-Air."

*   **Against SAMs:** Do not use planes. Use long-range artillery or specialized ground units to clear the SAMs first.
*   **Against Frigates/Cruisers:** Use submarines or your own naval destroyers. Strike fighters will be shredded by ship-based anti-air.
*   **Against Air Superiority:** Use your own Air Superiority fighters in a 5-stack to overwhelm them, or lure them over your own mobile anti-air units.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best way to use the World Fighters invasion guide for early-game expansion?**

A: Focus almost entirely on Strike Fighters for the first 48 hours. Most players start with basic infantry, which Strike Fighters can eliminate with zero casualties to your side. Once you have a small fleet, add two Air Superiority fighters to the stack to protect against any retaliatory air strikes.

**Q: Can stealth fighters be seen by regular radar?**

A: No, stealth fighters are invisible to standard radar and visual range. They can only be detected by "High-Level" radar (usually found on maxed-out SAM launchers) or other stealth units. This makes them perfect for the surprise attacks mentioned in this World Fighters invasion guide.

**Q: Is it worth building Aircraft Carriers in 2026?**

A: Generally, no. Aircraft carriers are expensive, require significant research, and are vulnerable to many low-cost counters. Most maps have enough land-based airfields to cover your invasion needs. Only consider them if you are conducting a massive trans-oceanic invasion where no friendly airbases exist.

**Q: How do I protect my planes while I am offline?**

A: Use the Patrol command over your own territory. If an enemy enters your patrol radius, your fighters will automatically engage. This is much safer than leaving them grounded at an airfield, where a single missile or ground unit could destroy your entire fleet while they are "parked."
