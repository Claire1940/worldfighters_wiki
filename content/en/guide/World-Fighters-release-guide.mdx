## Update 1.75: Quality of Life Overhaul

The primary objective of the 1.75 update was to address long-standing community requests regarding game flow and inventory management. For players who spend hours AFK-farming, the introduction of the "Auto Claim Rewards" feature is a game-changer. This system allows you to automatically collect rewards as they become available, though it is important to note that it does not currently auto-reset the reward tiers—manual intervention is still required for full resets.

Another significant addition is the Damage Test Dummy located directly in front of the leaderboards. This allows players to accurately measure their DPS without the interference of enemy mechanics or environmental hazards. By testing your builds here, you can determine if your current sword and accessory combination is truly optimized for high-level raids.

| Feature | Description | Impact Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Auto Claim Rewards** | Automatically collects earned rewards from the menu. | High (AFK farming) |
| **Damage Test Dummy** | Infinite health target used to measure real-time DPS. | Medium (Theorycrafting) |
| **Hide Gacha Animation** | Skips the summoning cinematic for faster unit acquisition. | High (Resource spending) |
| **Drop Billboards** | Displays potential loot next to game mode entrances. | Medium (Clarity) |

> **💡 Tip:** Always use the Damage Test Dummy after changing your passives. Small percentage increases in speed or power can result in millions of additional damage over the course of a long invasion.

## Mastering Speed Modifiers and Invasions

One of the most impactful mechanics introduced in the recent update is the Speed Modifier system for Dragon Defense and Tempest Invasions. Previously, these modes could feel sluggish, especially for high-level players who could one-shot early waves. Now, you can significantly accelerate the pace of these encounters.

To activate 2x speed, players must consume a specific key related to the invasion (such as a Slime Key or a Saying Key). While the 2x speed is accessible for most active players, the 3x speed modifier currently requires Gems. This creates a strategic choice: do you spend your Gems to save time, or do you stick to the key-based 2x speed for a more sustainable grind?

| Modifier Level | Cost | Recommended For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1x Speed** | Free | Beginners / Testing builds |
| **2x Speed** | 1 Game Mode Key | Daily grinding / Token farming |
| **3x Speed** | Gems | Speedrunning / Limited-time events |

When running the Slime Invasion at 2x speed, the enemies move and spawn twice as fast, effectively doubling your efficiency. This is particularly useful when hunting for Secret-rarity accessories like the Admiral Cape or the Fire Hat.

## Effective AFK Grinding Strategies

To truly excel in World Fighters in 2026, you must master the art of AFK (Away From Keyboard) grinding. The most efficient players utilize macros to automate their participation in the Dragon Arena and Dragon Defense modes. By setting up an auto-clicker or a more complex macro, you can accumulate thousands of tokens overnight.

Follow these steps to maximize your overnight gains:
1. **Stockpile Keys:** Spend an hour manually farming the Dragon Arena to collect at least 10-20 Saying Keys.
2. **Set Auto-Join:** Ensure your macro is positioned to click the "Auto" button within the Dragon Defense menu.
3. **Enable Speed Modifiers:** If you have an abundance of keys, ensure your macro activates the 2x speed modifier to finish waves faster.
4. **Vault Management:** Before starting your session, clear your inventory or set your auto-delete settings to keep only Mythic and Secret items.

Using this method, many players have reported being able to max out their secret gachas within 24 to 36 hours of total playtime. The tokens earned from these defenses are the primary currency for high-end progression.

## Equipment and Secret Rarity Drops

In the current version of the game, reaching the "Secret" rarity for your entire loadout is the ultimate goal. This includes your units, swords, and accessories. While swords are relatively straightforward to obtain through crafting and boss drops, accessories like the Admiral Cape and Cute Slime have much lower drop rates.

The Slime Invasion is currently the best place to farm for these elusive items. The drop table for this mode is varied, offering a 50% chance for two shards, which are essential for crafting and upgrading your gear.
