## Understanding the Passive System

Passives in World Fighters Simulator are unique modifiers that can be applied to your characters to enhance their combat capabilities or utility stats. Unlike temporary buffs, these passives are crafted using specific materials gathered throughout the game world, primarily through the Time Trial game mode.

To begin your journey with passives, you must navigate to the first world. Once there, head toward the back of the map near the developer area—look for the NPC labeled "Passive Punks." This station serves as the central hub for all your crafting needs. By interacting with the Index, you can view the specific requirements for every passive in the game.

> **💡 Tip:** Always check the Index before starting a Time Trial run so you know exactly which materials to prioritize during your grind.

## How to Obtain Crafting Materials

The backbone of the passive system is the Time Trial. This mode is the primary source for the various "mats" or materials required for crafting. The further you progress in a Time Trial, the higher the quantity and quality of the drops you will receive.

Common materials you will need to collect include:
*   **Wooden Katanas and Swords**
*   **Pirate Flags and Boat Rudders**
*   **Wooden Shields**
*   **Wanted Posters**
*   **Pirate Skulls**
*   **Diamonds** (Rare drop used for high-tier passives)

## Optimizing for Luck and Power

In World Fighters Simulator, two stats reign supreme: Damage (Power) and Luck. Depending on your current goals, you should swap your passives to match your activity.

### The Best Luck Passive
If you are aiming to summon secret or legendary units, the **Sea Captain** passive is widely considered the best choice for luck. It requires 10 Pirate Flags and 10 Boat Rudders. This is particularly useful for players who do not own the "Remote Summon" game pass, as it maximizes the efficiency of every summon session.

### The Best Damage Passive
For those looking to push through difficult boss stages or clear Time Trials faster, the passive requiring **6 Diamonds and 10 Wooden Katanas** is the gold standard. It provides a massive flat percentage increase to your unit's damage. This passive is often referred to as the "Meta Power" buff in the community.

> **Warning:** Crafting high-tier passives has a high material cost. Ensure you are applying them to your best units, as swapping them frequently can drain your Diamond reserves quickly.

## Advanced Strategy for Time Trial Grinding

To effectively use this **world fighters passive guide**, you need a steady stream of materials. Efficiency in Time Trials is the only way to keep up with the demand for Diamonds and high-quantity materials like Pirate Skulls.

1.  **Prioritize Speed:** Use units with high AoE (Area of Effect) to clear early waves instantly.
2.  **Stack Luck:** Even inside Time Trials, having a luck-based passive can occasionally influence the quality of drops from chests.
3.  **Group Up:** If the game allows for multiplayer Time Trials, grinding with a friend can help you reach higher waves where Diamond drop rates are more favorable.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the Passive Punks NPC?**

A: The Passive Punks are located in the first world. Head past the main spawn area toward the back of the map. You will see a group of NPCs near the developer area where you can access the Passive Index and crafting menu.

**Q: What is the most important material in this world fighters passive guide?**

A: Diamonds are the most valuable material. They are required for almost all top-tier Epic passives, including those that provide the highest damage buffs and health increases.

**Q: Can I lose my materials if a craft fails?**

A: Most of the recipes listed in the current version of the game have a 100% success rate if you provide the exact materials listed in the Index. However, always double-check the confirm screen before clicking.

**Q: Which passive should I get first as a beginner?**

A: Start with the **Wooden Sword** or **Warrior Guard** passives. They only require two common materials and provide a noticeable boost to your early-game survivability and damage output while you save up for more expensive recipes.
