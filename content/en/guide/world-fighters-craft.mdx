## How the Passive Crafting System Works

The passive station in World Fighters lets you convert farming drops into permanent passive effects for units. The system is simple on paper: collect materials, open the passive index, choose a recipe, and craft. In practice, efficiency depends on recipe order and farming route.

Here’s the core loop expert players follow:

1. Run Time Trial for material stock.
2. Return to the passive crafter in the early world hub.
3. Craft low-cost passives first to fill empty unit slots.
4. Pivot into epic/legendary passives once your material flow stabilizes.
5. Recraft only when a stronger passive creates a clear gain.

| Crafting Element | What It Does | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Materials** | Items like Diamonds, Wooden Swords, Pirate Flags | Gate every passive recipe |
| **Passive Index** | Recipe list with ingredient requirements | Prevents blind crafting |
| **Craft Confirm** | Converts materials into passive | Main progression trigger |
| **Equip Step** | Applies passive to selected unit | No equip = no benefit |

> **Tip:** Don’t dump rare ingredients into mid-tier passives if you’re one run away from a top-tier craft. Planning one recipe ahead saves hours.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even strong grinders lose efficiency through small errors. Here are the biggest traps in **world fighters craft** right now:

1. **Crafting without a target build**  
   Random passives feel productive but often delay meaningful upgrades.

2. **Spending Diamonds too early**  
   Diamond recipes are some of the highest leverage options. Don’t burn them on low-impact choices.

3. **Ignoring low-cost rare passives**  
   Cheap crafts are excellent for filling empty slots and smoothing progression.

4. **Not syncing crafting with summon plans**  
   Luck passives shine most when used before heavy summoning, not after.

5. **Forgetting equip confirmation**  
   Crafting alone does nothing until the passive is equipped on a unit.

If you correct those five habits, your **world fighters craft** efficiency improves immediately—even without better units.
