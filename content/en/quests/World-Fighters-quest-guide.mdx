## Getting Started: The First Island Quests

Every journey begins with the basics. On the first island, your primary objective is to complete the initial "Wanted Sign" quests. These tasks are designed to introduce you to the core loop of the game: killing enemies, collecting fruits, and upgrading your units.

The most important early-game milestone is the Buggy quest. You will need to defeat Buggy 25 times to satisfy the requirements for the next world key. While doing this, keep an eye out for "Rubber Fruits," which have a low drop rate but are essential for unlocking specific early-game units.

| Quest Target | Requirement | Reward / Unlock |
|---|---|---|
| **Buggy** | 25 Kills | Marine Fortress Key |
| **Kings** | Kill 5 | Unit Pity Progress |
| **Rubber Fruits** | Collect Drop (1%) | Luffy Unit Unlock |

> **💡 Tip:** Utilize the AFK Chamber as early as possible. It provides keys and gems that are vital for rolling the Haki and Sword banners without spending Robux.

## Marine Fortress and the Index System

Once you reach the Marine Fortress, the difficulty spikes. This is where the Index system becomes your best friend. By discovering five unique units on each map's banner and defeating specific enemies, you earn permanent power and damage buffs.

The Marine Fortress also hides a secret boss, often found in the castle or cage areas. Defeating these hidden foes is mandatory for completing your Index and maximizing your 10% power feats.

| System | Benefit | How to Optimize |
|---|---|---|
| **Index** | Permanent Power % Buff | Discover 5 units per world banner |
| **Haki** | Damage & Crystal Multiplier | Use keys from the AFK chamber to roll |
| **Swords** | Power Multiplier | Roll the Sword Banner for Epic/Legendary blades |

## Dressrosa: The Avatar Grind

The transition to Dressrosa marks the point where you should stop focusing solely on units and start focusing on your Avatar. Avatars provide massive power multipliers that dwarf standard unit buffs.

The Luffy Avatar is a fan favorite in 2026, offering a 6.01x power multiplier. To obtain him, you must complete the Wanted Poster requirements, which include farming the secret boss Kaido. Kaido has roughly a 6% drop chance for the items needed to unlock the avatar.

### Secret Boss Locations
*   **Zone 1:** Check the castle interior for hidden spawns.
*   **Dressrosa:** Kaido appears in a specific arena; he is essential for the "Wanted" questline.
*   **Dragon Verse:** Look for Broly, who serves as a high-tier power check for mid-game players.

## Dragon Verse and Ascension

In the Dragon Verse (Capsule Corps), you will encounter iconic characters like Goku and Vegeta. At this stage, your focus should shift toward Ascension. Ascending resets your current power but provides a massive boost to your base potential, making it easier to one-shot bosses like Ginyu and Frieza.

| Avatar / Unit | Multiplier | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Luffy Avatar** | **6.01x Power** | Dressrosa Wanted Poster |
| **Goku Unit** | 4.5x Power | Dragon Verse Banner |
| **Epic Sword** | 1.75x Power | One Piece Map Sword Banner |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not delete your Epic or Legendary swords when cleaning your inventory. The difference between a Rare (1.25x) and an Epic (1.75x) sword is significant when multiplied by your total power.

## Advanced Progression: Slime Verse and Beyond

The Slime Verse is currently one of the most challenging regions. Enemies here have massive health pools, and you will likely need to have ascended at least once or twice to clear the Demon Lord quest. This world introduces "Slime Power," a new stat that further complicates your build.

Unlike other simulators that feature complex separate builds, World Fighters Simulator focuses on a "Pure Punch" mechanic. This means your success depends entirely on stacking as many multipliers as possible from your Sword, Haki, Fighting Style, and Avatar.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find the secret boss in each world for the World Fighters quest guide?**

A: Secret bosses are usually hidden behind buildings or inside specific structures like castles. For example, in the Marine Fortress, the secret boss is located inside the castle cage. Defeating them is required to complete the Index for that world.

**Q: Is the AFK Chamber actually worth using?**

A: Yes. The AFK Chamber is the most efficient way for free-to-play players to earn keys for the Haki and Sword systems. It allows you to accumulate resources while you are away, which you can then spend to "speed-run" the kill quests in newer worlds.

**Q: What is the best Avatar to get for mid-game progression?**

A: The Luffy Avatar is highly recommended because it offers a 6.01x power multiplier. While Broly is technically stronger, the Luffy quest in Dressrosa is more accessible for players who are just reaching the mid-game threshold.

**Q: How does the Ascension system work?**

A: Ascension resets your current power to zero but grants you a permanent multiplier to all future power gains. It is generally best to ascend as soon as your progress slows down significantly on your current map.
