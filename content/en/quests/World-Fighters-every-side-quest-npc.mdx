## Understanding the Side Quest System in 2026

The developers have recently streamlined how players interact with side quests. One of the most significant changes is the implementation of the **Auto-Claim** feature. Previously, players had to return to the NPC to turn in their completed tasks; now, once you have met the requirements, the rewards are automatically applied to your character profile.

Side quests in World Fighters are distinct from the main progression line. They often require high-volume grinding, such as defeating bosses thousands of times or reaching specific waves in specialized game modes like the Tempest Invasion. Because these quests can be completed "off-screen" or while AFK, they are the perfect secondary objective while you focus on primary world progression.

> **Warning:** Side quest requirements do not track retroactively. You must interact with the NPC and accept the quest before your kills or "opens" begin counting toward the total.

## Every Side Quest NPC Location and Requirements

Finding **World Fighters every side quest npc** requires a bit of exploration, as many are tucked away behind buildings or inside caves that aren't immediately visible from the main path. Below is a breakdown of the four primary side quest NPCs found in the current build of the game.

### NPC 1: The Starter Power NPC
The first NPC is located very close to the initial spawn area, making it the most accessible for new players. This NPC is essential because it provides a massive 1.5x Power boost, which significantly speeds up early-game grinding.

*   **Location:** Look for a small structure or cave entrance near the starting zone. It is tucked away inside a building-like area that most players walk past during their first few minutes of play.
*   **Primary Objective:** Defeat the designated local enemies 750 times.

| Quest Metric | Requirement | Reward |

### NPC 2: The Coliseum Guardian
As you move into the more advanced sections of the first island, the requirements jump significantly. This NPC is located near the Coliseum, a landmark that is hard to miss but requires specific pathing to reach the quest-giver.

*   **Pathing:** From the first island's main hub, go straight, take a sharp left, and continue until you see the Coliseum area. The NPC is standing just outside the main arena entrance.
*   **Primary Objective:** This is a dual-requirement quest. You must utilize Rule Haki 1,000 times and defeat the Kaido boss 5,000 times.

> **💡 Tip:** 5,000 Kaido kills is a significant grind. It is highly recommended to set up an AFK macro overnight to complete this requirement efficiently.

### NPC 3: The Secret Boss Specialist
Located near the back of the map, this NPC is positioned close to where the Secret Boss spawns. This quest is notorious for its RNG-heavy requirements, specifically involving the acquisition of rare units.

*   **Location:** Head to the far back of the current world, past the standard boss spawns. The NPC is located in a lower-level area near the Secret Boss arena.
*   **Primary Objective:** Open 1,000 capsules/eggs and successfully pull the Broly unit four times.

| Requirement | Quantity | Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Capsule Opens** | 1,000 | Can be automated with "Auto-Open" |
| **Broly Units** | 4 | Requires high Luck stats |

### NPC 4: The Tempest Nation Commander
The final side quest NPC for the current update is found in Zone 6, also known as the Tempest Nation. This is an end-game NPC that requires players to have already reached a high level of power.

*   **Location:** In Zone 6, head toward the large central building. The NPC is hidden directly behind the building's rear wall.
*   **Primary Objective:** Defeat the "Guy" boss (Secret Boss) 5,000 times and reach Wave 25 in the Tempest Invasion game mode.

## Strategies for Completing Side Quests

When hunting for **World Fighters every side quest npc**, the discovery is only half the battle. The actual completion of these quests requires a strategy to manage the high volume of kills and RNG pulls.

1.  **Prioritize the 1.5x Power Multiplier:** Do not leave the first zone until you have finished the 750-kill quest. That multiplier affects all future gains, making the 5,000-kill grinds in later zones much more manageable.
2.  **Optimize Luck for NPC 3:** Since NPC 3 requires you to pull four Broly units, ensure you have your Luck potions active and your Luck-based equipment equipped before you start your 1,000 opens.
3.  **Tempest Invasion Readiness:** Reaching Wave 25 in Tempest Invasion is a gear check. If you find yourself dying before Wave 20, pivot back to farming the Secret Boss for better units before attempting the quest again.

For more detailed stats on unit scaling, you can check the [official World Fighters community hub](https://www.roblox.com/games/14236123456/World-Fighters-Simulator) to see how your current power compares to the requirements for Zone 6.

## Advanced Pathing for Zone 6
Zone 6 can be confusing due to its verticality and large structures. To find the Tempest Nation NPC quickly, follow these steps:
1.  Enter Zone 6 and stay on the main paved path.
2.  Look for the tallest building in the center of the city.
3.  Do not enter the building; instead, circle around the right side.
4.  The NPC is standing in the shadows of the back alleyway.

By following this route, you can secure the final quest and begin your 5,000 Guy kills immediately. Remember that the "Guy" character is often considered a secret boss, so his spawn rate might be lower than standard zone bosses.
